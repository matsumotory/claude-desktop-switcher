Zero-Impact Isolation

Isolate Claude Desktop Environments.

Customize the separation level of your profiles. This macOS utility unifies the management of both your Claude Desktop App and Claude Code (CLI) environments.

Unified Control of
GUI and CLI

While Claude Desktop and Claude Code are separate tools, this utility manages their backend profiles as one. Access the same isolated environment whether you use the menu bar for the GUI or the terminal for the CLI.

Customizable Separation

Define exactly what to share and what to isolate. For example, you can completely separate your login sessions and past chat histories between 'Work' and 'Personal' profiles, ensuring your data never crosses over.

Zero System Impact

Your Mac's system settings are never overwritten. Active profiles are applied strictly locally—only to the Claude Desktop app launched via this tool or your specific terminal tab—ensuring it safely coexists with your usual development environment.

Designed for real workflows.

Adapt the level of isolation to match how you actually work on a single machine.

[Case 01] Strict Personal / Org Separation

The Need: You must ensure that personal projects and company data never cross-pollinate, preventing accidental chat history leaks.
The Solution: Use "Complete Isolation" to physically separate data directories and configuration files. What happens in one profile stays completely invisible to the other.

[Case 02] Dev / Research Sharing

The Need: You want separate billing accounts for development and research, but you want them to share the same plugins and global configurations.
The Solution: Use "Flexible Sharing" to sync specific configuration files across profiles while keeping authentication data strictly isolated.

[Case 03] Multi-Account Scaling

The Need: One account simply isn't enough, and you need to seamlessly juggle a second or third account throughout the day.
The Solution: Switch profiles from the menu bar with a single click. Access entirely separate environments without the friction of constantly logging in and out.

Flexible Sharing

03

Terminal Context Switching
